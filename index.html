A Tribute Page To The Olympic Glories Of India

The Olympic Games is an important international event that features summer as well as winter sports.The first "modern" Olympics happened in 1896 in Athens, Greece. Athletes participate in the Olympics Games to represent their respective countries.The celebration of the Games includes many rituals and symbols, such as Olympic flag and torch, as well as the opening and closing ceremonies. The first, second, and third-place finishers in each event receive, respectively, gold, silver, and bronze medals. It is held in every four years. So this year Tokyo which is located in Japan was choosen to host "Olympics 2020".

NEERAJ CHOPRA

  • Neeraj Chopra who was born on 24 December 1997 is an Indian track and field athlete who competes in the javelin throw.He is Junior Commissioned Officer(JCO) in the Indian Army.
  • He is ranked as World No: 2 by the World Athletics Association.
  • He is the first track and field athlete to win a gold medal for India at the Olympics. In his debut at the "2020 Tokyo Olympics", Chopra won the gold medal on 7 August 2021 with a throw of 87.58 m in his second attempt.
  • He is also the first track and field athlete from India to win at the IAAF World U20 Championships, where in 2016 he achieved a world under-20 record throw of 86.48 m, becoming the first Indian athlete to set a world record.
  • He is the youngest ever Indian who has won a gold medal at an indivisual event

RAVI KUMAR DAHIYA

  • Ravi Kumar Dahiya, also known as Ravi Kumar,is an Indian freestyle wrestler who won a silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics in the 57 kg category.

LOVLINA BORGOHAIN

  • Lovlina Borgohain who was born on 2 October 1997 is an Indian amateur boxer and an Olympic medal winner.
  • She won a bronze medal at the 2020 Summer Olympics (Tokyo,Japan) in the Women's welterweight event, becoming only the third Indian boxer to win a medal at the Olympics.
  • She is the first female athlete and the second boxer from Assam to represent the state in the Olympics.
  • In 2020, she became the sixth person from Assam to receive Arjuna Award.

P.V.SINDHU

  • Pusarla Venkata Sindhu who was born on 5 July, 1995 is an Indian professional badminton player. Over the course of her career, Sindhu has won medals at multiple tournaments including Olympics.
  • She won a bronze medal at the Summer 2020 Olympics of Tokyo.
  • She is the first Indian to become the Badminton World Champion and only the second individual athlete from India to win two consecutive medals at the Olympic Games.

MIRABAI CHANU

  • Saikhom Mirabai Chanu who was born 8 August 1994 is an Indian weightlifter. She won the silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics in Women's 49 kg category.
  • She was awarded the Padma Shri by the Government of India for her contributions to the sport.
  • Her biggest achievement came in 2017, when she won the gold medal at World Weightlifting Championships held in Anaheim, California.
  • Chanu has also won the silver medal in the women's 48 kg weight class at the 2014 Commonwealth Games, Glasgow; she went on to break the games record en route to the gold medal at the 2018 edition of the event held in Gold Coast.
